What is the LibreNova License (LNL)?

The LibreNova License (LNL) v1.1 is an open-source software license designed to promote software freedom while preventing monopolization and proprietary exploitation. It ensures that a significant portion of a project remains open and accessible, while still allowing commercial use and modifications. Under LNL, at least 35% of the total codebase must remain open-source, including core functional components. The license also enforces transparency and prevents large corporations from restricting access to open-source projects.

Key Features of LNL

  • Permissive: Allows for both open source and partly proprietary use. (min. 35% of code of the software applied must be open source.)
  • Simple: Easy to understand and apply.
  • Flexible: Suitable for a wide range of projects and use cases.

Why Use LNL?

Using LNL allows you to leverage the benefits of open source while maintaining a massive level of control over your code. It encourages contributions and collaboration from the community.

What are the benefits of LNL?

    For Open-Source Developers:
  • Protects open-source projects – prevents companies from taking and fully privatizing software.
  • Ensures transparency – at least 35% of the code must always be publicly available.
  • Freedom to fork and modify – developers can build upon existing projects without restrictions.
  • Community-friendly – encourages contributions and collaboration.
    • For Businesses & Commercial Users:
  • Allows commercial use – including modifications and sales, as long as the license terms are met.
  • Clear SaaS rules – software can be offered as a service (SaaS) if contributions are made to the open-source community.
  • Prevents vendor lock-in – companies cannot restrict access or monopolize open-source projects.
    • For the Open-Source Community:
  • Prevents monopolization – large companies cannot privatize the software without also contributing.
  • Guarantees accessibility – even if a project is abandoned, forks must remain publicly available.
  • Encourages innovation – developers can freely innovate without the risk of corporate takeover.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I use LNL in a closed-source project?

Yes, but at least 35% of your code must remain open-source under the same license.

What happens if I violate the 35% open-source requirement?

Your license could be revoked, and legal action may be taken by the LibreNova Foundation.

Can a company with a market dominance of over 70% use LNL-licensed software?

Only if they release a 100% open-source fork under the same license.

Is SaaS covered by this license?

Yes. If you deploy the software as SaaS, you must either contribute modifications back or ensure interoperability with other open-source projects.
